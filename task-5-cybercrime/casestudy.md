# Case Study: UPI / Online Payment Fraud

**What is UPI / Online Payment Fraud?**  
UPI fraud is a type of cybercrime where fraudsters trick people into transferring money to fake accounts or steal banking credentials through digital payment apps. It’s becoming increasingly common as more people use UPI for day-to-day transactions.

**How it mainly Happens:**  
1. The victim receives a call, text, or social media message claiming to be from their bank or a common UPI app.  
2. The message warns about a suspicious transaction or urges immediate account verification.  
3. The attacker may share a fake UPI link, QR code, or ask for the victim’s OTP or UPI PIN.  
4. Trusting the message, the victim enters the requested details or approves a transaction.  
5. The attacker uses this information to transfer funds, often draining the account or making unauthorized payments.

**Who is Usually Targeted:**  
College students, young adults, and online shoppers are frequent targets, especially those new to digital payments or less cautious with OTPs and UPI PINs.

**Consequences:**  
Victims may face immediate financial loss, stress, and a long process to recover money. Fraud can also affect credit records or lead to repeated unauthorized transactions. Awareness, vigilance, and verifying messages before responding are key to staying safe.
